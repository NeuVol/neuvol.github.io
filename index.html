Edge-Assisted Immersive Streaming with Neural Radiance Fields

Overview

Holographic communication offers an interactive user experience by delivering immersive content that depicts 3D scenes, allowing users to change their viewpoint and view direction freely. Traditional content representations such as point clouds and meshes have been widely studied for immersive video streaming, but their discrete nature limits photo-realistic rendering and degrades user experience.

This project explores neural radiance fields (NeRF), a novel implicit representation that models 3D scenes continuously using neural networks. NeuVol aims to build a real-time immersive video streaming system with neural content representation that is:

  • Resilient to packet loss and rendering artifacts
  • Feasible within current Internet bandwidth
  • Capable of achieving low end-to-end latency

The research introduces innovations in streaming resiliency, bandwidth optimization, and latency reduction, integrating them into a comprehensive edge-assisted framework. A proof-of-concept will be developed and evaluated in real-world environments.
